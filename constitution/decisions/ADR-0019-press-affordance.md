# ADR-0019 — Press affordance

source: `GAP-U-interaction-affordance`, quantified by the pattern screen at ~30 pressability
rows (21 `cursor: pointer`, 12 outside recipes, plus the bare-control bundle).

The gap report's larger arc asked whether interaction affordance becomes capability
vocabulary (`pressable / draggable / expandable / editable`) with behaviour left to
JavaScript. Options weighed:

- Capability words on the state plane, `selectable`-shaped. Rejected by the constitution's
  own law: state controls nothing (P7-4d), and the pressability evidence is a painted
  property — a capability that emits CSS would be a category error.
- Component-owned, wait for the full arc. Rejected for the same reason as ever: twelve
  identical non-recipe declarations is the admission bar several times over.
- The read/write split, placed where each side belongs: `pressable` is a skin treatment
  owning `cursor` (the read side — what the element invites); event wiring, keyboard, and
  focus management stay JavaScript (the write side). Narrow admission: `pressable` only,
  the family reserved. Chosen.

`user-select` deliberately stays out of `pressable`: its evidence is recipe-only
(selectable-group, radio labels), and R-SKIN-10 keeps recipe bundles whole.

Introduces ruling: R-SKIN-17. Resolves GAP-U-interaction-affordance (the pressable half is
ruled; further members remain evidence-gated within the ruled family).
